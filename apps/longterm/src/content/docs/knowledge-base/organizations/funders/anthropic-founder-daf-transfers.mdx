## Overview

All seven Anthropic co-founders have pledged to donate 80% of their equity. [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/01/27/anthropic-billionaire-cofounders-ceo-dario-amodei-giving-away-80-percent-of-wealth-fighting-inequality-ai-revolution/) At current valuations, that's \$39-59B. The question is whether any of this equity will be transferred to donor-advised funds *before* Anthropic's expected 2026-2027 IPO.

This matters for two reasons. First, pre-IPO DAF transfers behaviorally lock in the commitment before the wealth becomes liquid and psychologically "real." Historical <EntityLink id="giving-pledge">Giving Pledge</EntityLink> data shows only 36% of deceased pledgers met their commitments. [IPS](https://ips-dc.org/report-giving-pledge-at-15/) Second, any founder equity that is eventually *sold* rather than *donated directly* incurs ~37% combined capital gains tax in California — tax that a DAF transfer would avoid entirely.

The central estimate in this analysis is **\$2-12B** in pre-IPO founder DAF transfers, driven primarily by <EntityLink id="dario-amodei">Dario</EntityLink> and <EntityLink id="daniela-amodei">Daniela Amodei</EntityLink>. The other five founders have no documented philanthropic infrastructure or connections that would predict pre-IPO action.

## What Should We Expect to Happen?

The strongest candidate. 43rd signatory of the Giving What We Can pledge, early GiveWell supporter, former housemate of <EntityLink id="holden-karnofsky">Holden Karnofsky</EntityLink>. His January 2026 essay ["The Adolescence of Technology"](https://www.darioamodei.com/essay/the-adolescence-of-technology) frames the 80% pledge as a considered moral commitment, not a performative gesture. He has the values, the connections, and presumably access to philanthropic tax advisors who would recommend a pre-IPO transfer. [EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/53Gc35vDLK2u5nBxP/anthropic-is-not-being-consistently-candid-about-their)

Working against: he's running one of the world's most important AI companies during a period of rapid growth. Philanthropic tax planning may not be a priority. He might also prefer to retain flexibility on timing and cause allocation rather than locking equity into a DAF now.

**<EntityLink id="daniela-amodei">Daniela Amodei</EntityLink> (President)** — Probability of pre-IPO DAF transfer: **35-55%**

Married to <EntityLink id="holden-karnofsky">Holden Karnofsky</EntityLink>, co-founder of GiveWell and former CEO of Open Philanthropy. Karnofsky joined Anthropic in January 2025. [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/02/13/anthropic-hired-president-daniela-amodei-husband-ai-safety-responsible-scaling/) This represents the single strongest bridge between Anthropic founder wealth and the effective giving ecosystem. **<EntityLink id="chris-olah">Chris Olah</EntityLink>** — Probability of pre-IPO DAF transfer: **10-25%**

Pioneer in neural network interpretability whose research focus aligns with <EntityLink id="technical-ai-safety">technical AI safety</EntityLink>. If Clark's philanthropic interests lean toward AI governance and advocacy, DAFs would be a poor vehicle for his goals, reducing the likelihood of pre-IPO transfers.

The default path — "I'll figure it out after the IPO" — is much easier.

### What About After IPO?

Pre-IPO is the focus of this page, but it's worth noting the post-IPO dynamics:

| Period | What Happens | Implication |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| **IPO to lock-up expiry** (6-12 months) | Founders can't transfer or sell shares | Dead period — no philanthropic action possible |
| **First year post-lock-up** | Founders gain liquidity; wealth feels "real" | Critical behavioral window — giving decisions made here often stick |
| **Years 2-10** | Gradual selling, foundation creation, DAF transfers | <EntityLink id="giving-pledge">Giving Pledge</EntityLink> data suggests wealth grows faster than giving |
| **Years 10-30** | Estate planning, final pledge fulfillment (or not) | 36% fulfillment base rate for deceased pledgers |

The risk is that founders who don't act pre-IPO enter the "I'll do it later" spiral documented in Giving Pledge data, where wealth compounds faster than giving for decades. [IPS](https://ips-dc.org/report-giving-pledge-at-15/)

## Potential Loss Analysis

### The Three Sources of Loss

Loss from failing to transfer equity to DAFs pre-IPO comes from three distinct channels:

**1. Tax waste (on shares sold rather than donated)**

When founders eventually sell shares for personal use (living expenses, diversification, other investments), they pay ~37% combined capital gains tax in California:

| Tax Component | Rate |
|---------------|------|
| Federal long-term capital gains | 20% |
| Net Investment Income Tax (ACA surtax) | 3.8% |
| California state income tax | 13.3% |
| **Combined** | **≈37%** |

Every dollar of stock sold rather than donated directly to a DAF or charity loses ≈\$0.37 to taxes. If a founder keeps 20% (the non-pledged portion) and sells it, the tax hit on the retained portion alone is \$0.5-0.8B per founder.

But the real tax loss comes when founders *partially fulfill* pledges by selling stock first and donating cash — or when they sell more than 20% for personal use. Each share sold rather than donated directly costs 37 cents per dollar.

**2. Pledge failure (shares never donated)**

The <EntityLink id="giving-pledge">Giving Pledge</EntityLink> fulfillment rate provides the base rate. Only 36% of deceased pledgers met even a 50% threshold. Living pledgers have grown 166% wealthier since signing. [IPS](https://ips-dc.org/report-giving-pledge-at-15/) If Anthropic founders follow similar patterns:

| Fulfillment Rate | Amount Donated | Amount Not Donated | "Lost" vs. 80% Pledge |
|-----------------|----------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| 80% (full pledge) | \$39-59B | \$0 | \$0 |
| 60% | \$29-44B | \$10-15B | \$10-15B |
| 40% (Giving Pledge base rate) | \$20-30B | \$19-29B | \$19-29B |
| 20% | \$10-15B | \$29-44B | \$29-44B |

Pre-IPO DAF transfers don't guarantee the money reaches high-impact causes (DAF donors retain advisory discretion), but they do irrevocably remove the money from the founder's personal balance sheet.

**3. Timing cost (delayed deployment)**

If AI safety funding is time-sensitive — as many in the field argue — then money deployed in 2027 is worth more than money deployed in 2035 or 2045. This is hard to quantify but potentially the largest source of loss. [EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/rRBaP7YbXfZibSn3C/front-load-giving-because-of-anthropic-donors)

### Per-Founder Loss Scenarios

For a single founder with 2.5% stake (\$8.75B at \$350B valuation):

| Path | Donated | Tax Paid | Kept | "Lost" to Charity |
|------|---------|----------|------|-------------------|
| **Pre-IPO DAF (80%)** | \$7.0B (to DAF) | \$0 | \$1.75B | \$0 |
| **Post-IPO direct stock donation (80%)** | \$7.0B | \$0 | \$1.75B | \$0 |

| **Giving Pledge base rate (36%)** | \$3.15B | \$0.8B* | \$4.8B | \$3.85B |
| **Never donates** | \$0 | \$2.0B* | \$6.75B | \$7.0B |

*Tax estimates assume the retained/sold portion incurs capital gains. Actual tax depends on the mix of selling and donating stock directly.

### What Pre-IPO DAF Transfers Specifically Prevent

| Without Pre-IPO Transfers | With Pre-IPO Transfers |
|---------------------------|------------------------|
| 80-90% of pledged equity remains uncommitted at IPO | 10-30% of equity locked into DAFs before liquidity |

## Practical Obstacles

Even founders who want to make pre-IPO DAF transfers face real frictions:

| Obstacle | Severity | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| **Board approval** | Moderate | Anthropic board must approve private stock transfers; precedent exists via employee matching |
| **DAF sponsor acceptance** | Moderate | Not all DAF sponsors accept illiquid private stock; specialty sponsors like Fidelity Charitable do |
| **Qualified appraisal** | Low-Moderate | IRS requires independent appraisal for deductions on non-publicly-traded stock |
| **409A valuation gap** | Low | 409A valuations for private stock can differ significantly from FMV, affecting deduction size |
| **30% AGI deduction limit** | Low | Donations of non-publicly-traded stock to public charities are limited to 30% of AGI per year (excess carries forward 5 years) |
| **Founder time** | High | Setting this up takes meaningful attention from people running a rapidly scaling company |

Notably, Anthropic's existing employee matching program has already navigated most of these obstacles. The board has approved stock transfers to DAFs, DAF sponsors are accepting Anthropic stock, and the valuation infrastructure exists. The marginal cost for founders to use the same pipeline is substantially lower than building from scratch.
