## Overview

<EntityLink id="anthropic">Anthropic</EntityLink>'s rapid valuation growth—from \$550 million in May 2021 to \$350 billion by January 2026—has created what may become the largest single source of longtermist philanthropic capital in history. [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/07/anthropic-funding-term-sheet-valuation.html) All seven co-founders have pledged to donate 80% of their equity, and many early employees hold Giving What We Can pledges or have expressed intentions to donate significantly. [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/01/27/anthropic-billionaire-cofounders-ceo-dario-amodei-giving-away-80-percent-of-wealth-fighting-inequality-ai-revolution/)

However, significant uncertainty surrounds when this capital will become liquid (pending IPO), whether pledges will be honored at scale, and what causes will ultimately receive funding. Historical precedent suggests young tech wealth rarely translates into sustained philanthropic giving, making execution risk a central concern.

## Funding History

### Early Rounds (2021-2022)

Anthropic's seed and Series A rounds were dominated by EA-connected investors:

| Round | Date | Amount | Key Investors |
|-------|------|--------|---------------|
| Seed | Early 2021 | Undisclosed | <EntityLink id="jaan-tallinn">Jaan Tallinn</EntityLink>, <EntityLink id="dustin-moskovitz">Dustin Moskovitz</EntityLink>, Eric Schmidt, James McClave |
| Series A | May 2021 | \$124M | Jaan Tallinn (lead), Dustin Moskovitz, Center for Emerging Risk Research |
| Series B | April 2022 | \$580M | Spark Capital, others |

Jaan Tallinn, co-founder of Skype and a prominent EA movement supporter, led the Series A at a \$550 million pre-money valuation. [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-raises-124-million-to-build-more-reliable-general-ai-systems) Dustin Moskovitz, co-founder of Facebook and funder of <EntityLink id="coefficient-giving">Coefficient Giving</EntityLink> (formerly <EntityLink id="open-philanthropy">Open Philanthropy</EntityLink>), participated alongside other safety-minded investors.

### Strategic Tech Investments (2023-2024)

The company's later rounds brought massive capital from strategic technology partners:

**Google**: Invested approximately \$3 billion total, including \$300 million in late 2022 (for 10% stake), \$2 billion in October 2023, and \$1 billion in early 2025. Google now owns approximately 14% of Anthropic. [Verdict](https://www.verdict.co.uk/us-doj-google-anthropic-partnership/)

**Amazon**: Invested \$8 billion total across three rounds—\$4 billion in September 2023, \$2.75 billion in March 2024, and \$4 billion in November 2024. Amazon Web Services became Anthropic's "primary cloud and training partner." Amazon remains a minority investor without a board seat.

### Series C-F (2023-2025)

| Round | Date | Amount | Valuation | Lead Investors |
|-------|------|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Series C | May 2023 | \$450M | — | Spark Capital, Google, Salesforce |
| Series D | December 2023 | \$2B | — | Various |
| Series E | March 2025 | \$3.5B | \$61.5B | Lightspeed Venture Partners |

The Series F round brought institutional investors including Qatar Investment Authority, Ontario Teachers' Pension Plan, GIC (Singapore), Goldman Sachs, TPG, and T. Rowe Price. [Anthropic](https://www.anthropic.com/news/anthropic-raises-series-f-at-usd183b-post-money-valuation)

### Latest Round (January 2026)

Anthropic signed a term sheet for \$10 billion at a \$350 billion valuation, led by Coatue and GIC. [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/07/anthropic-funding-term-sheet-valuation.html) This nearly doubled the September 2025 valuation of \$183 billion.

## EA-Connected Investors and Founders

### Founder Donation Pledges

All seven Anthropic co-founders—<EntityLink id="dario-amodei">Dario Amodei</EntityLink>, <EntityLink id="daniela-amodei">Daniela Amodei</EntityLink>, <EntityLink id="chris-olah">Chris Olah</EntityLink>, Tom Brown, Jack Clark, Jared Kaplan, and Sam McCandlish—have pledged to donate 80% of their equity. [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2026/01/27/anthropic-billionaire-cofounders-ceo-dario-amodei-giving-away-80-percent-of-wealth-fighting-inequality-ai-revolution/)

At current valuations, each co-founder's stake is estimated at approximately \$3.7 billion (down from ~6% to 2-3% each due to dilution). The 80% pledge from all seven founders could direct **\$20+ billion** toward philanthropy.

### Individual EA Connections

**Dario Amodei (CEO)**: 43rd signatory of the Giving What We Can pledge; wrote guest posts for GiveWell around 2007-2008; lived in a group house with <EntityLink id="holden-karnofsky">Holden Karnofsky</EntityLink> and <EntityLink id="paul-christiano">Paul Christiano</EntityLink>. [EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/53Gc35vDLK2u5nBxP/anthropic-is-not-being-consistently-candid-about-their)

**Daniela Amodei (President)**: Married to Holden Karnofsky, co-founder of GiveWell and former CEO of Open Philanthropy. This connection creates a direct bridge between Anthropic wealth and the EA funding ecosystem.

**Chris Olah**: Pioneer in neural network interpretability; his research focus aligns with <EntityLink id="technical-ai-safety">technical AI safety</EntityLink> priorities.

### Early Investors

**Jaan Tallinn**: Co-founder of Skype; one of EA's most significant funders. Has "poured millions into effective altruism-linked nonprofits and AI startups." Led Anthropic's Series A and remains an angel investor. [Semafor](https://www.semafor.com/article/11/21/2023/how-effective-altruism-led-to-a-crisis-at-openai)

**Dustin Moskovitz**: Through Good Ventures, moved a \$500 million Anthropic stake into a nonprofit vehicle to reinvest returns. [Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/11/10/meet-the-millennial-meta-cofounder-and-his-wife-who-are-giving-away-20-billion/) The couple has donated over \$4 billion total, including \$600 million+ in 2025 alone.

### Early Employees

Additional Anthropic employees have committed to donating shares potentially worth billions, with Anthropic offering 3:1 donation matching for early employees' gifts to 501(c)(3) organizations. [EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/rRBaP7YbXfZibSn3C/front-load-giving-because-of-anthropic-donors) Amanda Askell, an early employee, was the 67th signatory of the GWWC pledge.

## The FTX Stake

FTX invested approximately \$500 million for a 13.56% stake in Anthropic before its November 2022 bankruptcy. Due to subsequent funding rounds, this diluted to approximately 7.84% by 2024. [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2024/03/25/ftx-estate-sells-majority-stake-in-startup-anthropic-for-884-million.html)

The FTX bankruptcy estate sold the stake in two tranches:

1. **March 2024**: Sold two-thirds of the stake for \$884 million. Primary buyer was Mubadala (UAE sovereign wealth fund) at ~\$500 million; other buyers included Jane Street, HOF Capital, Ford Foundation, and Fidelity.
2. **Later 2024**: Sold remaining 15 million shares at \$30/share for \$452 million to G Squared and others.

Total recovery: approximately \$1.3 billion from the \$500 million investment. These proceeds went to FTX creditors rather than EA-aligned causes.

## Key Uncertainties

### Will Pledges Be Honored?

Historical evidence suggests caution:

> "Maintaining altruistic giving after sudden wealth is really difficult. There are surprisingly few cases of young people (under 40) giving away millions after a cash windfall." [EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/rRBaP7YbXfZibSn3C/front-load-giving-because-of-anthropic-donors)

**Positive indicators:**
- Founders have long EA involvement (GWWC pledges since 2010s)
- Daniela Amodei's marriage to Holden Karnofsky creates accountability
- Public commitment increases reputational cost of reneging
- Anthropic's stated safety mission aligns with EA priorities

**Risk factors:**
- Wealth changes priorities and social circles
- 80% is an unusually high pledge percentage
- No legal enforcement mechanism for donations
- "Paper money" until IPO—circumstances may change

### What Causes Will Receive Funding?

Even if pledges are honored, cause allocation remains uncertain:

**Likely to receive more:**
- AI safety research (natural alignment with Anthropic's mission)
- Technical alignment work
- <EntityLink id="governance-policy">AI governance and policy</EntityLink>

**Uncertain:**
- Global health and poverty (traditional EA focus)
- Animal welfare
- Other longtermist causes

One analysis notes: "AI safety likely receives more Anthropic employee funding, while animal welfare and global health may face different dynamics." [EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/rRBaP7YbXfZibSn3C/front-load-giving-because-of-anthropic-donors)

### Valuation Risk

Even with a \$350 billion valuation, significant downside scenarios exist:

| Scenario | Founder Wealth (Each) | Total Philanthropic Potential |
|----------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| IPO at \$350B | ~\$7-10B | \$40-55B (at 80%) |
| IPO at \$100B | ~\$2-3B | \$11-17B (at 80%) |
| Major correction | \$500M-1B | \$2.8-5.6B (at 80%) |
| Company failure | ~\$0 | ~\$0 |

The company's unusual public-benefit corporation structure may also affect valuation if public markets discount the governance constraints.

## Strategic Implications for EA Funding

### Scale Comparison

The EA movement has historically directed approximately \$1 billion annually. Anthropic-derived funding could represent a 10-50x increase in available capital if pledges are honored at scale. [EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/H8SqwbLxKkiJur3c4/preparing-for-a-flush-future-work-giving-and-conduct)

### Front-Loading vs. Waiting

Some argue current donors should give now rather than wait for Anthropic wealth:

> "A \$100k gift represents 9% of current funding versus only 1% of projected future funding... organizations become less constrained by money than capacity." [EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/rRBaP7YbXfZibSn3C/front-load-giving-because-of-anthropic-donors)

Counter-argument: Coordination may be valuable to avoid redundant capacity building.

### Absorption Capacity

Whether the AI safety and broader EA ecosystem can productively absorb billions in additional funding remains unclear. Rapid funding increases historically create challenges around:
- Talent constraints
- Organizational scaling
- Grant evaluation capacity
- Potential for reduced rigor
