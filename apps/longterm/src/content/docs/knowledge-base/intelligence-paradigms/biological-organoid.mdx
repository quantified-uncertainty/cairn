## Overview

Biological/organoid computing uses **actual biological neurons** as computational substrates rather than silicon. This includes brain organoids (miniature brain-like structures grown from stem cells), neuron-computer interfaces, and "wetware" computing.

The field made headlines with **DishBrain** (2022), which showed neurons in a dish learning to play Pong. While fascinating from a scientific perspective, this approach is **far from TAI-relevant** due to massive scaling challenges.

Estimated probability of being dominant at transformative AI: **&lt;1%**

### Scale Comparison

| System | Neurons | Status | Intelligence |
|--------|---------|--------|--------------|
| **Current organoids** | ~1-3 million | Achieved | None meaningful |
| **DishBrain** | ~800,000 | Achieved | Learned Pong (barely) |
| **Mouse brain** | 70 million | Not achieved | Simple mammal |
| **Human brain** | 86 billion | Far future | Human-level |

## Research Landscape

### DishBrain (Cortical Labs, 2022)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **What** | Neurons on multi-electrode array play Pong |
| **Achievement** | Learning demonstrated in biological system |
| **Significance** | First proof of "organoid intelligence" |
| **Limitations** | Simple task, inconsistent learning |

## Why It's Unlikely to Lead to TAI

### Fundamental Challenges

| Challenge | Severity | Explanation |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| **Scale** | CRITICAL | Need 10,000x+ more neurons |
| **Reliability** | HIGH | Biological systems are noisy |
| **Speed** | HIGH | Neurons are ~million times slower than silicon |
| **Reproducibility** | HIGH | Each organoid develops differently |
| **Maintenance** | HIGH | Requires constant biological support |
| **Interface** | HIGH | Getting information in/out is hard |

### Comparison with Silicon AI

| Factor | Biological | Silicon AI |
|--------|------------|------------|
| Development speed | SLOW | FAST |
| Scalability | VERY HARD | Relatively easy |
| Reproducibility | LOW | HIGH |
| Cost per computation | HIGH | LOW and decreasing |
| Current capabilities | Pong (barely) | Superhuman at many tasks |

## Trajectory

### Why It Might Matter Eventually

1. **Biological computation is efficient** - Brains use ~20W
2. **Proof of principle** - DishBrain shows learning is possible
3. **Neuroscience advances** - Understanding growing
4. **Niche applications** - Drug testing, disease modeling

### Why It Probably Won't Lead to TAI

1. **Silicon AI is winning decisively** - GPT-4 vs. DishBrain
2. **Scaling is monumentally hard** - Biology doesn't follow Moore's law
3. **Reproducibility issues** - Can't copy a brain organoid like copying weights
4. **Interface problems** - Getting data in/out is bottleneck

## Key Uncertainties

1. **Could biological computing leapfrog silicon?** Extremely unlikely but not impossible.

2. **What are the ethical boundaries?** As organoids grow more complex, ethics become more pressing.

3. **Could insights from biocomputing help AI?** Understanding biological learning might inform artificial systems.

4. **Hybrid approaches?** Perhaps biological components integrated with silicon.
